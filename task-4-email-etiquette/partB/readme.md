Poor digital communication can often lead to misunderstandings and serious consequences. For example, imagine a student sending an email to a professor using informal language, without a proper subject line, and with unclear content. The professor might misunderstand the request or even ignore the email, considering it unprofessional. This could result in missed deadlines or lost opportunities.

Such situations can be avoided by following proper digital communication etiquette. The student should use a clear subject line, maintain a respectful tone, and structure the message properly. Proofreading the email before sending is also important to avoid errors or confusion. By communicating clearly and professionally, students can ensure their message is understood correctly and create a positive impression in academic and professional environments.
